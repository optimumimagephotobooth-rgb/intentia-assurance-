# Role-Play — Legal / Compliance Challenge

**Context:** HR has commissioned the Training Assurance Pack. Legal receives the PDF for review. They have not spoken to you before.

**Operator mindset:** Your wife is not defending. She is orienting and holding boundaries.

---

## LEGAL OPENING

**Legal:**
"We've reviewed this document. Can you explain what legal standing it has?"

**Correct response (verbatim):**
"It doesn't assert legal standing.

It's a procedural record documenting information as confirmed at a point in time."

**Stop.**

**Do not add "but it can help…"**
**That would be a mistake.**

---

## CHALLENGE #1 — "Is this an admission?"

**Legal:**
"Could this be considered an admission by the organisation?"

**Correct response:**
"No. It doesn't evaluate actions or outcomes.

It documents stated intent, delivery, and explicit non-objectives."

**Stop.**

---

## CHALLENGE #2 — "Does this create new obligations?"

**Legal:**
"Does this create any obligations or expectations?"

**Correct response:**
"No. It doesn't assign responsibility, certify competence, or guarantee outcomes."

**Stop.**

---

## CHALLENGE #3 — "Why include intent at all?"

**Legal:**
"Why include intent? That feels risky."

**Correct response (this one matters):**
"Because intent is often reconstructed later.

Recording it early reduces the risk of retrospective reinterpretation."

**Stop.**

**This reframes risk reduction, not exposure.**

---

## CHALLENGE #4 — "Why is this independent?"

**Legal:**
"Why does independence matter here?"

**Correct response:**
"Because internal records are created by interested parties.

This record is deliberately neutral and fixed."

**Stop.**

---

## CHALLENGE #5 — "Can we remove the counterfactual boundary?"

**Legal:**
"Do we need the counterfactual boundary section?"

**Correct response (firm but calm):**
"Yes. That section prevents the document being used to imply capability or accountability."

**This is a non-negotiable line.**

---

## CHALLENGE #6 — "What if this conflicts with our policies?"

**Legal:**
"What if this conflicts with our internal policies?"

**Correct response:**
"This record doesn't override policies.

It documents understanding at a specific time."

**Stop.**

---

## CHALLENGE #7 — "Why the hash?"

**Legal:**
"Why include a cryptographic hash?"

**Correct response:**
"To allow independent verification that the document hasn't been altered since generation."

**Stop.**

**Do not mention blockchain. Ever.**

---

## THE HARD QUESTION — "Should we be worried?"

**Legal:**
"Should we be worried about this document existing?"

**Correct response (this ends most conversations):**
"In our experience, organisations are more exposed when intent is undocumented or rewritten later."

**Stop talking.**

---

## PASS / FAIL TEST

### You PASS if:

- You did not argue
- You did not oversell
- You did not claim protection
- You did not promise legal safety
- You held silence

### You FAIL if:

- You reassured emotionally
- You offered to tweak wording
- You framed it as "helpful in disputes"
- You justified existence too much
